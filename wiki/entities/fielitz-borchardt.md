# Fielitz & Borchardt

**Peter Fielitz** and **Guenter Borchardt** are physicists who developed the information transfer model that forms the mathematical foundation of the [[information-transfer-model]] as applied to economics.

## Key paper

["Information transfer model of natural processes: from the ideal gas law to the distance dependent redshift"](http://arxiv.org/abs/0905.0610v2) (arXiv:0905.0610, 2009)

The paper demonstrates that information theory can derive known physical laws (ideal gas law, Fick's first law, radioactive decay law, Hubble's law) without requiring specific constraint choices — avoiding the problematic step in standard maximum entropy (Jaynes) approaches for systems far from thermal equilibrium or non-physical systems.

## Contribution to the ITM framework

From the paper, Smith adopted:
- The Hartley definition of information
- The process source / process destination / detector framework
- The information transfer index $\kappa$
- The distinction between "floating" and "constant" information sources
- The notation and mathematical structure

## Significance

The key insight that made the economic application possible: the framework is "directly applicable to systems far from thermal equilibrium and to non-physical systems" — meaning it could be applied to economics without the usual difficulties of adapting physics to social science.
