# arXiv Paper: Information Equilibrium as an Economic Principle

The formal paper crystallizing the entire [[information-transfer-model]] framework. Published as arXiv:1510.02435.

- **arXiv**: [1510.02435](http://arxiv.org/abs/1510.02435)
- **SSRN**: [2894072](http://ssrn.com/abstract=2894072)
- **Author**: [[jason-smith]]
- **Draft completed**: August 21, 2015 (500+ blog posts of development)
- **Edits from**: [[fielitz-borchardt]] and Tom Brown

## Structure

The paper covers the full framework in five sections:

1. **Introduction** — information theory applied to economics
2. **Information equilibrium** — the core formalism
   - 2.1 [[supply-and-demand]] derivation
   - 2.2 Physical analogy ([[thermodynamic-analogies]])
   - 2.3 Alternative motivation
3. **Macroeconomics** — applications
   - 3.1 [[ad-as-model]]
   - 3.2 Labor market and Okun's law
   - 3.3 IS-LM model and [[interest-rates]]
   - 3.4 Price level and [[inflation]]
   - 3.5 [[solow-growth-model]]
   - 3.6 Note on constructing models
4. **Statistical economics** — [[entropic-forces]] and emergent properties
5. **Summary and conclusion**

## Significance

This represents the transition from "blog as working paper" to formal academic publication. The paper distills ~2.5 years of blog exploration into a rigorous framework that was subsequently cited and engaged with by other researchers. It's the canonical reference for the information equilibrium approach to economics.
