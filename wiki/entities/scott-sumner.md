# Scott Sumner

Economist and leading proponent of **market monetarism** and **NGDP level targeting**. Blogs at The Money Illusion. One of [[jason-smith]]'s most frequent interlocutors.

## Key positions

- Monetary policy is always effective if expectations are properly managed
- NGDP level targeting via futures markets would provide optimal monetary policy
- QE is temporary and won't cause lasting inflation
- Supply-side reforms (Reagan/Thatcher) drove faster growth

## ITM critique

Smith's [[information-transfer-model]] directly challenges Sumner's framework:

- The [[information-trap]] theorem proves that **any** market-based monetary targeting (including NGDP targeting) eventually hits a regime where $\partial P/\partial \text{MB} = 0$
- Supply-side reforms didn't drive growth — inherited low [[information-transfer-index]] κ set the trajectory
- Monetary offset cannot always offset fiscal policy — it fails in the information trap
- New market channels (NGDP futures) start with poor information transfer capacity (see [[money-as-bandwidth]])

Despite disagreements, Smith engages respectfully and analyzes Sumner's LS-MS model within the ITM framework.
