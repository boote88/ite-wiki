# Paul Krugman

Nobel laureate economist, New York Times columnist. One of the key voices Smith engages with, often in partial agreement.

## Relationship to the ITM

Krugman's observation that supply and demand curves are hard to derive from first principles but everyone uses them anyway was one of the sparks that ignited the ITM project. Smith took this as a challenge: *can* you derive S&D from first principles? The answer was yes, via [[information-equilibrium]].

On the [[information-trap]], Smith largely agrees with Krugman's liquidity trap analysis while showing it's actually more general — the trap can occur at any interest rate, not just at the [[zero-lower-bound]]. Krugman is right that monetary policy becomes ineffective and fiscal policy becomes essential, but for information-theoretic reasons rather than the standard IS-LM story.

Smith uses Krugman & Wells' *Macroeconomics* textbook principles as the starting point for restating economics in the [[information-equilibrium]] framework — replacing utility maximization with maximum entropy throughout.
