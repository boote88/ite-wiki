# Two parameters vs twenty-nine (2015-01-08)

[Original post](../../raw/2015/01/two-parameters-vs-twenty-nine.md)

Head-to-head comparison between the [[information-transfer-model]] (2 parameters) and the NY Fed DSGE model (29 parameters) for inflation prediction.

## The chart

![ITM vs NY Fed DSGE inflation prediction](<../media/2015-01/basic us price level m0 interst rates -compare FRB-US model- smoothed -corrected for quarterly avg-.png>)

Monthly data is following the NY Fed's lower 90% contour while sitting inside the ITM's 70% contours. The ITM is winning the prediction contest with 27 fewer parameters.

## Significance

This comparison crystallizes the ITM's central claim: a simple information-theoretic model with minimal parameters outperforms elaborate DSGE models with many more degrees of freedom. The [[information-equilibrium]] approach (maximum ignorance) produces better predictions than models built on detailed microfoundations and rational expectations.

The comparison was initiated in [September 2014](../../raw/2014/09/) and continues through 2015.
