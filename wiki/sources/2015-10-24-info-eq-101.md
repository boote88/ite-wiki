# Info EQ 101 (2015-10-24)

[Original post](../../raw/2015/10/info-eq-101.md)

The most complete single-post derivation of the entire [[information-equilibrium]] framework — written as a chalkboard lecture with hand-drawn diagrams. Derives everything from first principles: widget boards → differential equation → supply & demand → AD-AS → money → inflation regimes.

## Step 1: Widget boards and information matching

Two boards with widgets. Demand widgets on one, supply widgets on another. Each widget reveals $\log \sigma$ bits of information. Setting demand information equal to supply information gives the core equation.

![Widget board setup](<../media/2015-10/2ab21625-e098-44ab-a8c8-c62c85e1ca89.jpg>)

![Derivation of the differential equation](<../media/2015-10/3364723a-7107-45b7-93e9-478dab2a6499.jpg>)

The ratio $k = \log \sigma_d / \log \sigma_s$ is the [[information-transfer-index]]. The derivative $dD/dS$ is defined as an abstract price $P$. Key properties: marginal relationship, satisfies homogeneity of degree zero.

Notation: $P : D \rightleftarrows S$

## Step 2: Non-ideal information transfer

![Non-ideal: information loss as KL divergence](<../media/2015-10/6e99e77a-dea9-4cf4-bf7c-1f7bd73ce3f0.jpg>)

When distributions don't match, $I_D > I_S$ — [[non-ideal-information-transfer]]. The differential equation becomes an inequality. Via Gronwall's inequality, ideal solutions become bounds on the non-ideal case.

## Step 3: General equilibrium solution

![General equilibrium: power law](<../media/2015-10/6d4b93c0-d140-4826-bd82-6399578c9204.jpg>)

Both S and D vary together → power law: $D \sim S^k$. If supply grows exponentially at rate σ, then demand grows at $k\sigma$ and price at $(k-1)\sigma$.

## Step 4: Partial equilibrium → supply and demand curves

![Partial equilibrium: supply and demand baths](<../media/2015-10/e1f4bbb4-a61d-46e0-ab48-fb1071a3ab35.jpg>)

![Supply and demand curve derivation](<../media/2015-10/284df07d-8e7e-4eda-ac3e-3069ab06ab53.jpg>)

Hold one side constant ("bath") while other adjusts. Supply bath → demand curve (price down, demand up). Demand bath → supply curve (price up, supply up).

![Marshallian S&D diagram recovered](<../media/2015-10/98c0e06a-3f9e-47f3-a710-3664fc711b8f.jpg>)

Recovers the standard Marshallian diagram with κ relating to price elasticities.

## Step 5: Non-ideal information transfer region

![Non-ideal region: price in orange triangle](<../media/2015-10/3c8bf976-bd39-4e1e-85e8-c35cb725fd0f.jpg>)

Under [[non-ideal-information-transfer]], solutions become bounds. Price can appear anywhere in the orange triangle. Normal growth punctuated by recessions (serious non-ideal transfer) and supply/demand shocks.

## Step 6: AD-AS model

![AD-AS from same framework](<../media/2015-10/770cc6e6-491c-4361-b138-3b6d6ae3efee.jpg>)

Same equations, but widgets represent aggregate demand and supply. The partial equilibrium solution gives the SRAS curve; the general equilibrium solution gives the LRAS curve.

## Step 7: Introducing money via chain rule

![Money mediates AD→AS via chain rule](<../media/2015-10/0e77cb32-a2a5-4c7c-bc49-aa733b3e7771.jpg>)

AD in IE with money, money in IE with AS. Chain rule gives a new IE relationship between AD and money with composite index. The AS relationship drops out in equilibrium.

## Step 8: Inflation regimes from κ

![Inflation regimes from the information transfer index](<../media/2015-10/ed438af3-81ae-4bfa-a9f8-738ca5889959.jpg>)

![High vs low κ limits](<../media/2015-10/089dff57-c799-45b4-ab92-187280cccff0.jpg>)

If $k$ large → $\nu \approx \pi$ → real growth small compared to inflation → **high inflation limit** ([[quantity-theory-of-money]] regime). If $k \approx 1$ → price level roughly constant → **low inflation limit** ([[information-trap]] regime). The entire spectrum from QTM to liquidity trap is a single parameter.
