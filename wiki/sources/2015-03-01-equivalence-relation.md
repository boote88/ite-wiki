# Information equilibrium is an equivalence relation (2015-03-01)

[Original post](../../raw/2015/03/information-equilibrium-is-equivalence.md)

A formal proof that [[information-equilibrium]] ($A \cong B$) satisfies all three properties of an equivalence relation:

1. **Reflexivity**: $A \cong A$ with $k = 1$
2. **Symmetry**: $A \cong B \implies B \cong A$ with $k' = 1/k$
3. **Transitivity**: $A \cong B$ and $B \cong C \implies A \cong C$ with $k = ab$ (via chain rule)

## Significance

This is what makes the unified ITM model possible (see [[2015-02-25-simultaneous-models]]). If NGDP is in information equilibrium with M0, and M0 is in IE with the price level, then NGDP is in IE with the price level. The models don't need to be solved independently — they form a consistent equivalence class.
