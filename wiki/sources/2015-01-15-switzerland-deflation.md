# Switzerland depreciated their currency and still had deflation (2015-01-15)

[Original post](../../raw/2015/01/switzerland-depreciated-their-currency.md)

The Swiss National Bank (SNB) pegged their currency to the Euro to devalue it — but still got deflation. Then they abandoned the peg on January 15, 2015, after the devaluation failed to produce inflation or spur growth.

## ITM interpretation

This outcome is exactly what the [[information-trap]] predicts:
- Switzerland is in a liquidity trap (κ near 1, identified in [August 2014](../../raw/2014/08/monetary-regime-change.md))
- [[exchange-rates]] don't have an immediate link to inflation — they decouple in the information trap
- Currency depreciation moved the exchange rate but couldn't move the price level

[[scott-sumner]] argued exchange rate movement indicates looser monetary policy, but the ITM shows this is a non-sequitur when κ → 1: the price level is insensitive to monetary changes regardless of what the exchange rate does.
