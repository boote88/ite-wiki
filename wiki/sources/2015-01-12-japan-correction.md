# Updates for Japan (and a major correction) (2015-01-12)

[Original post](../../raw/2015/01/updates-for-japan-and-major-correction.md)

Smith corrects a fitting error in the Japan price level model. The corrected model reveals that **Abenomics' inflation was just mean reversion**, not a policy success.

## The correction

The original fit used only 2005–2013 data. Fitting to the full 1994–2013 period shows Abenomics started at the **largest negative deviation** from the model. The subsequent above-average inflation was expected to return to trend regardless of policy.

## Significance

- Abenomics didn't cause Japan's brief inflation — it was mean reversion from the largest negative deviation in the model
- The corrected model tracks even non-seasonally-adjusted data with seasonal fluctuations
- Japan remains in the [[information-trap]] — structural deflation continues regardless of monetary policy (see [[cross-country-comparisons]])
- Smith notes: "I could have predicted the rise in inflation everyone thinks was a result of Abe but instead seems to be just mean reversion"
