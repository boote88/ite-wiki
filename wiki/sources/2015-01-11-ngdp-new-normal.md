# NGDP predictions and the new normal (2015-01-11)

[Original post](../../raw/2015/01/ngdp-predictions-and-new-normal.md)

The ITM predicts NGDP for Q4 2014 and argues that post-2008 slow growth is the "new normal" — not a temporary deviation from trend.

## The prediction comparison

Three competing models for NGDP:

1. **"Old normal"**: log-linear extrapolation from 1980s–present (excluding dot-com and housing bubbles) → higher growth, expects return to trend
2. **"New normal"**: log-linear extrapolation from 2010 → slower growth as permanent
3. **ITM prediction**: based on M0 path fed into the NGDP-M0 theoretical relationship → closely matches "new normal"

## Key insight

The ITM and "new normal" are nearly identical in the near term — both predict slowing growth won't reverse. The "old normal" (return to post-1980s averages) would reject the ITM model. This connects to [[secular-stagnation]]: the economy is near the [[information-trap]], so growth rates are structurally lower.

The ITM predicts the recent US growth spurt won't be sustained — lower NGDP numbers expected in 2015.
