# Keynesianism and NGDP growth (2015-01-11)

[Original post](../../raw/2015/01/keynesianism-and-ngdp-growth.md)

Uses the ITM to settle the Wren-Lewis vs. [[scott-sumner]] debate on fiscal policy effectiveness. The answer: **fiscal policy works**, at least in the ITM.

## Method

Uses Robert Waldmann's "paleo-Keynesian" metric for government stimulus: total government spending + 0.5 × federal deficit (implying multipliers of 1.5 for federal, 1.0 for state/local, -0.5 for taxes minus transfers). Combined with the ITM's ability to extract NGDP shocks and the counterfactual trend from the NGDP-M0 path.

The key advantage: the ITM accounts for post-crisis "stagnation" (the new normal of slowing NGDP growth), so fiscal effects can be cleanly separated from the structural trend.

## Key finding

Even the simplistic first-order Keynesian model looks better than a monetary offset model (which would show a flat red line at zero). This is consistent with the ITM prediction that fiscal policy works in the [[information-trap]] because $\partial P/\partial \text{NGDP} \neq 0$ even when $\partial P/\partial \text{MB} \approx 0$ — monetary offset is impossible when monetary policy is ineffective.
