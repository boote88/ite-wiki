# Powerful evidence for the information transfer model (2015-01-19)

[Original post](../../raw/2015/01/powerful-evidence-for-information.md)

A striking visualization: plot actual data (annual average M0 growth vs NGDP growth) on top of the ITM's theoretical curves. The data falls exactly in the predicted triangle pattern.

## The key chart

![NGDP vs M0 growth with data overlay](<../media/2015-01/ite us price level and ngdp-m0 path -with data-.png>)

Rainbow curves show the theoretical response of NGDP to changes in M0 for 1965 (purple) to 2015 (red) in 10-year steps. Data points are annual averages of M0 and NGDP growth rates, colored by time period.

## Why this is powerful

The data falls in a triangle mapped out by the theoretical lines, with points fading chronologically from purple to red along the predicted curves. This demonstrates $\text{NGDP} \sim k \log M0$ with $k$ falling over time — regardless of your theoretical priors.

**What other theories would predict:**
- **Pure QTM**: a single line (not a triangle)
- **Expectations-based theory**: a complicated relationship depending on the exact sequence of shocks and central bank announcements — no reason for this triangle pattern
- **Simple martingale** (past inflation predicts future): could achieve a similar result, but doesn't *explain* anything

## Significance

This chart is arguably the most direct visual evidence for the [[information-transfer-model]]. It shows the [[information-transfer-index]] κ declining over time as a deterministic fact of the data, not a model assumption. The [[quantity-theory-of-money]] is a limiting case (a single line), while the ITM captures the full evolution.

Smith notes: "How can you be *accurate* when there are no quantitative predictions? What on Earth does *accurate qualitative analysis* mean?" — a pointed critique of schools that claim empirical success without quantitative predictions.
