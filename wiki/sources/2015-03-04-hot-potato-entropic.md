# The hot potato effect is an entropic force (2015-03-04)

[Original post](../../raw/2015/03/the-hot-potato-effect-is-entropic-force.md)

The monetarist "hot potato effect" — where injections of high-powered money cascade through exchanges until the price level adjusts — is reinterpreted as an [[entropic-forces|entropic force]]. No utility maximization needed.

## The standard story

[[scott-sumner]] calls the hot potato effect the *sine qua non* of monetary economics: people already hold as much money as they want, so extra cash is "sold" in a cascade until everyone re-equilibrates at a higher price level. Sumner himself admits: "attempts to understand money at the level of the representative consumer fail."

## The entropic force reinterpretation

The additional money randomly moves around (prices randomly fluctuate) until the new **most likely configuration** is reached by chance. More "bad deals" (accepting too-high prices) are made possible because the additional money opened up new regions of state space.

In thermodynamic language: adding energy to a system opens up higher-momentum phase space, raising the temperature.

Smith created an animation (on [YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=44XiXBDx5rI)) showing money injection into one sector "thermalizing" — spreading uniformly through random exchanges until the median meets the new average.

## Key distinction

**No human behavior is required — even at the macro level.** Agents aren't thrown off a "desired" equilibrium. They don't "want" to get rid of excess holdings. The new equilibrium is simply the new most likely state, and agents with above-average holdings tend to reduce them through random exchanges.

"Utility maximizing agents? *I have no need of that hypothesis.*"

## Connection to [[entropic-forces]]

This is another instance of macro phenomena emerging from statistics rather than optimization:
- [[sticky-prices]]: entropy cost of coordination
- Recessions: spontaneous entropy decrease (coordination)
- Hot potato effect: thermalization to new equilibrium distribution
- All without microfoundations
