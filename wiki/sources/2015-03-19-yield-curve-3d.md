# The yield curve in 3D (2015-03-19)

[Original post](../../raw/2015/03/the-yield-curve-in-3d.md)

3D visualization of the ITM's theoretical yield curve — long (10-year) and short (3-month) interest rates over time.

## Charts

### Theoretical yield surface — data

![3D yield curve data](<../media/2015-03/interest rate -yield-.png>)

### Theoretical yield surface — model

![3D yield curve model](<../media/2015-03/interest rate -yield model-.png>)

The difference between the long (far) and short (near) sides is entirely due to central bank reserves — see [[currency-vs-reserves]]. Short rates are lower because reserves are non-zero.

Points where short-term rate data are *above* the theoretical surface signal an approaching recession. These are also when yield curves tend to invert (short > long rates).

### Animated: short rate "flapping" around the theoretical curve

![Yield curve flapping animation](<../media/2015-03/flapping.gif>)

Data minus theory for the log interest rate — the short-rate end "flaps in the wind" while the long end stays anchored. The flapping represents [[non-ideal-information-transfer]] deviations from the [[plucking-model]] ceiling.
