# 2013 H2: Framework Maturation (99 posts)

July–December 2013 transforms the [[information-transfer-model]] from a theoretical curiosity with one empirical success into a comprehensive macroeconomic framework with cross-country validation, formal theorems, and explanatory power across economic history.

## Major developments by month

### July (28 posts): Universalization and shocks
- **κ-σ space**: normalized framework allowing cross-country comparison on the same graph; US, Japan, EU converge on a "ridge" where monetary policy fails
- **Information trap emerges**: beyond a critical MB size, further monetary expansion *decreases* the price level — resolving Japan's puzzle of decades of expansion producing no inflation
- **κ as unit of account**: $\kappa(\text{NGDP}, \text{MB}) = \log(\text{NGDP}/c_0) / \log(\text{MB}/c_0)$ — money is simultaneously medium of exchange and unit of account
- **Nominal shock extraction**: methodology to decompose NGDP into expected (given monetary policy) vs. actual; isolates a ~15% shock in the Great Recession
- **AD-AS as 3D surface**: price level as function of (MB, NGDP), explaining why same stimulus has different effects in 1990 vs. 2010
- **Prediction out to 2030**: first long-range forecast using the framework

### August (12 posts): IS-LM derivation and phase transitions
- **IS-LM derived from information theory**: goods market (IS) and money market (LM) as information transfer markets — not just a teaching tool but theoretically grounded
- **Information trap formalized**: when κ → 1, ∂P/∂MB → 0, creating a "ridge" on the price surface. More general than Keynes' liquidity trap — can occur at any interest rate
- **Phase transition theory**: fitting pre-WWII and post-WWII separately works; the economy underwent a phase transition around WWII/Bretton Woods. Depression and current recession: crossing the same threshold in opposite directions
- **LS-MS model**: labor market and money market via information transfer, deriving Okun's law
- **Scott Sumner's model** analyzed and critiqued within ITM framework
- **Non-normative econophysics**: ITM succeeds because it doesn't import normative assumptions (utility maximization)

### September (23 posts): Information trap theorem and hyperinflation
- **Information trap theorem (formal proof)**: given market P:NGDP→MB with floating source/destination and $\kappa(\text{NGDP}, \text{MB})$, there exists an MB* where $\partial P/\partial \text{MB} = 0$. Any market-based monetary policy targeting eventually fails.
- **Hyperinflation as exit mechanism**: escaping the trap requires MB to become a *constant* (non-market) information destination — central bank prints regardless of price signals. This is what happened in the 1940s (WWII, rate pegging).
- **κ-driven history of economic thought**: entire history of US macro from 1900–2013 explained by κ trajectory. Paradigm shifts (classical → Keynesian → monetarist → New Keynesian) track κ moving through different regimes.
- **Unit of account as number base**: κ → 1 means MB approaches the scale of NGDP. The monetary base *is* the number system (base ~283 in 2013 US).
- **Information trap ≠ ZLB**: formally proved distinct. EU in information trap at 1–2% interest rates.
- **Fisher equation breaks down** in information traps
- **Top 10 successes** listed: first self-assessment of the framework's track record

### October (19 posts): Cross-country analysis and historical revisionism
- **100th post retrospective**: the 1970s as a continuous trajectory, not an anomaly — oil shocks are minor perturbations on the expected path
- **Supply-side reforms debunked**: Reagan/Thatcher reforms didn't drive growth; inherited low κ set the trajectory from 1945 onward
- **Cross-country comparisons**: UK, Australia, Canada mapped in ITM framework; κ predicts monetary effectiveness ranking
- **Sticky wages derived**: from P:NGDP→NW market, not microfoundations
- **Phillips curve reinterpreted**: not a structural trade-off but mean reversion after recessions
- **Neo-Wicksellian indeterminacy resolved**: κ determines which outcomes obtain at ZLB
- **Predictions to 2030** for price level and interest rates

### November (9 posts): Three ideas and secular stagnation
- **"Three Ideas" framework** explicitly articulated — the complete ITM in three building blocks:
  1. S&D with price as detector → P = (1/κ)(D/S)
  2. Multiple interacting markets using same framework
  3. Unit of account effect — κ endogenous to MB and NGDP
- **Secular stagnation reframed**: not mysterious, just the information trap. Developed economies exhausted the "low-hanging fruit" of monetary policy.
- **140 years of UK data** validated against ITM — single fit across monetary regimes
- **NAIRU as κ ratio**: natural unemployment ≈ κ_LS / κ_U
- **Sweden as ideal**: near-constant κ ≈ 0.67, pure quantity theory regime

### December (8 posts): Plucking model and efficiency
- **Plucking model formalized**: RGDP growth bounded by IS = ID (100% information transfer efficiency); recessions are "plucks" below the bound
- **Information transfer efficiency**: observable relative changes in IS/ID ratio; Great Moderation = operating near bound; Great Recession = sharp efficiency drop
- **Deflationary monetary expansion**: the price surface has a peak; on the right side, ∂P/∂MB < 0 — same QE can be inflationary or deflationary depending on starting position
- **No static equilibria**: proved that no path exists where interest rate and inflation are simultaneously constant
- **Microfoundations critique deepened**: information theory provides trend explanation; microfoundations unnecessary for macrotheory, just as molecular physics unnecessary for thermodynamics
