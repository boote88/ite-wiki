# Do the different market models work simultaneously? (2015-02-25)

[Original post](../../raw/2015/02/do-different-market-models-work.md)

A key validation: all the ITM's separate models — aggregate demand, labor market, [[solow-growth-model]], IS-LM — **work simultaneously** as a unified system with NGDP as the common information source.

## The model diagram

![Unified ITM model diagram](<../media/2015-02/models.png>)

NGDP (N) sits at the center as the common information source. Arrows show information flow to different destinations (monetary base, currency, labor, capital) with prices/detectors on the arrows. The Solow model (blue) and labor market (red) share the labor variable L but can be solved simultaneously.

## Key insight

[[information-equilibrium]] is an **equivalence relation**: if A is in IE with B, and B is in IE with C, then A is in IE with C. This means the separate models (price level, interest rates, labor market, growth) aren't independent ad-hoc fits — they form a consistent system derivable from a single information source (NGDP).

This addresses a common critique: that the ITM is just curve-fitting separate models independently. It isn't — the models share parameters and can be solved as one system.
