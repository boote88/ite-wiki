# Entropy and the Walrasian auctioneer (2015-03-18)

[Original post](../../raw/2015/03/entropy-and-walrasian-auctioneer.md)

The Walrasian auctioneer — the theoretical construct ensuring no single agent affects prices in perfect competition — is just **entropy maximization**.

## The connection

Stephen Williamson (quoted by Simon Wren-Lewis): "these microfoundations would do a pretty poor job at explaining the behaviour of any particular individual, but instead model common tendencies that emerge within large groups of individuals." Smith's response: "I don't think there is a more perfect way of summing up the concept of [[entropic-forces]]."

### Entropy as tâtonnement

![Entropy climbing to maximum — particles equalizing](<../media/2015-03/boxes.gif>)

Walras' tâtonnement (search for equilibrium) is like entropy climbing to maximum: particles move between left and right sides until they equalize. No single particle strongly affects the result, but collectively they find the maximum entropy solution.

### Price adjustment distribution

![Price change distribution with inflation target](<../media/2015-03/ite adjustment.png>)

Each box = a price change state. The central bank sets the mean of this distribution at the inflation rate. Moving any single box (red arrow) has ~zero effect on the distribution. In the limit $N \to \infty$, no single agent impacts prices — the desired effect of the Walrasian auctioneer, achieved without the auctioneer.

## Key insight

The equilibrium price is an **emergent quantity** made from millions of price changes. The auctioneer is unnecessary — entropy does the same job. See [[entropic-forces]], [[microfoundations-critique]].
