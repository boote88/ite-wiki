# Nominal rigidity is an entropic force (2015-03-17)

[Original post](../../raw/2015/03/nominal-rigidity-is-entropic-force.md)

Empirical data flatly contradicts the two mainstream micro explanations of [[sticky-prices]] — but the [[entropic-forces]] view explains it perfectly.

## The mainstream approaches

- **Menu costs**: changing a price has a cost → price changes should be infrequent and biased toward large jumps
- **Calvo pricing**: only a fraction of firms can change prices each period → price changes should be infrequent

Both predict prices are sticky **individually**.

## What the data actually shows

Among 60,000 observed prices:

![Price change frequency distribution 1](<../media/2015-03/price1.gif>)

![Price change frequency distribution 2](<../media/2015-03/price2.gif>)

*Oh...* Nothing stops individual prices from changing. **Prices are sticky in aggregate but not individually.** The mainstream approaches are "flatly contradicted by data."

## The entropic force explanation

Thousands of individual prices fluctuate wildly, but the aggregate level barely moves — just like individual molecules bouncing around at high speed while the average pressure stays constant. The aggregate stickiness emerges from entropy: the overwhelming number of microstates consistent with the current macro price level.

## Implication

There is **no microeconomic explanation of nominal rigidity** — it's a purely emergent macro phenomenon. This flies in the face of the microfoundations approach to the Lucas critique. See [[microfoundations-critique]], [[entropic-forces]].
