# What if US inflation had been 2% since the 1960s? (2015-02-01)

[Original post](../../raw/2015/02/what-if-us-inflation-had-been-2-since.md)

A counterfactual: if the US had maintained a 2% inflation target from 1960, the economy would be **only a tenth of its current size**. The 1970s "stagflation" that everyone says was terrible actually turbocharged NGDP growth.

## Key insights

- The economy shifted toward 2% inflation starting in the 1980s under Volcker — the actual curve moves to become parallel with the counterfactual
- The big kink in the economic growth rate around 1980 (higher before, lower after) appears to be simply a shift from a policy regime that didn't focus on inflation to one that did
- The 1970s inflation, while disruptive, produced the NGDP growth that made the US economy as large as it is today
