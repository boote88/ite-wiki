# Mr. Short Rate Risin' (2015-01-19)

[Original post](../../raw/2015/01/mr-short-rate-risin.md)

If we've seen "peak QE" (the monetary base has peaked and will stay flat), then short-term interest rates are **already rising** — before the Fed formally announces anything.

### Short-term interest rates already rising under peak QE

![Short rates already rising](<../media/2015-01/ite interest rates us -already rising-.png>)

Model in blue, data in green/yellow. If the monetary base stays flat while NGDP continues to grow, short rates rise mechanically in the ITM — no Fed announcement needed.

## Key insight

In the [[information-transfer-model]], short-term rates are determined by r:NGDP→MB (see [[currency-vs-reserves]]). If MB stops growing while NGDP continues its trend, the ratio changes and rates rise. The Fed's "decision" to raise rates may just be acknowledging what the model already shows happening.
