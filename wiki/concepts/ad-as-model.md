# AD-AS Model in the ITM

The [[information-transfer-model]] extends directly from micro supply-and-demand to the **aggregate demand / aggregate supply** framework:

| Micro S&D | AD-AS |
|---|---|
| Demand $Q^d$ (info source) | Aggregate demand |
| Supply $Q^s$ (info destination) | Aggregate supply |
| Price $P$ (detector) | Price level |

All prior ITM results carry over: [[supply-and-demand]] curves, [[sticky-prices]], [[non-ideal-information-transfer]].

## Key result: the modern AD-AS picture

With "rational expectations" (observed price as lower bound on AS), the model produces:

- **Below equilibrium output**: prices are sticky, resources idle (unemployment, empty storefronts, robots turned off). Policies to boost AD restore output without significant [[inflation]].
- **Above equilibrium output**: AD boosts only raise the price level without increasing output (pure inflation).
- This gives a **sharp right angle** in the AS curve at the current price level — recovering the modern macro consensus.

The classical view (purely vertical AS, instant price clearing) corresponds to assuming ideal information transfer always holds ($I_{Q^d} = I_{Q^s}$), which Smith considers unlikely.

## Relationship to IS-LM

The [[is-lm-model]] is the parallel construction with the interest rate (rather than the price level) as the shared detector. AD-AS and IS-LM are two views of the same underlying ITM machinery — different "prices" tracking different aspects of the same information equilibrium structure. The IS-LM analysis additionally reveals that the IS curve changes shape over decades as κ evolves, producing the monetarist-→Keynesian-→liquidity-trap progression visible in the data.
