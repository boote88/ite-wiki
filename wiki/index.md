# Index

## Concepts

- [[information-transfer-model]] — Core framework: price detects information transfer from demand (source) to supply (destination)
- [[supply-and-demand]] — Derivation of S&D curves from ITM using constant/floating information sources
- [[non-ideal-information-transfer]] — Information loss produces sticky prices and market imperfections
- [[thermodynamic-analogies]] — Economics↔thermodynamics mapping (price↔pressure, demand↔energy, supply↔volume)
- [[ad-as-model]] — Aggregate demand/supply as information transfer; recovers modern macro with sticky prices
- [[information-transfer-index]] — κ = log(NGDP/c₀)/log(MB/c₀): central parameter governing monetary policy effectiveness
- [[information-trap]] — Regime where ∂P/∂MB = 0; monetary policy fails. More general than ZLB
- [[three-ideas]] — Complete ITM in three building blocks: S&D, multiple markets, unit of account effect
- [[plucking-model]] — Business cycles as deviations from information-efficiency ceiling (IS = ID)
- [[currency-vs-reserves]] — M0 (currency) controls prices; MB (base) controls interest rates
- [[entropic-forces]] — Macro phenomena as statistical inevitability: prices, wages, recessions, money, bank runs
- [[information-equilibrium]] — Maximum-entropy / "ignorance equilibrium": equivalence relation, satisfies Lucas critique
- [[economic-potentials]] — Thermodynamic potentials for the economy: temperature T~1/κ, micro vs macro forces
- [[emergent-representative-agent]] — Rational behavior emerges from high-dimensional geometry, not from assuming utility
- [[quantity-theory-of-labor]] — NGDP and inflation in IE with civilian labor force; capital exponent zero; demographics is destiny
- [[dynamic-information-equilibrium]] — DIEM: constant log-derivative + logistic shocks; major new framework from 2017
- [[information-bottleneck]] — Prices destroy information (not transmit it); resolves Hayek and Stiglitz; GAN analogy

- [[exchange-rates]] — Derived from relative price levels; decouple from inflation in the information trap

- [[interest-rates]] — Two-rate model (long→M0, short→MB); yield curve, recession prediction, rate forces

- [[predictions]] — Comprehensive track record: forecasts, model competitions, cross-country validations, corrections

- [[unobservables]] — Natural rate, NAIRU, velocity, expectations: all circular. ITM provides calculable replacements
- [[recessions]] — Coordination failure; aperiodic avalanches; Great Recession as Fed-induced; recovery regularity
- [[labor-market]] — Two markets (wage + employment); Okun's law mechanical; absolute vs relative stickiness
- [[is-lm-model]] — Derived from information theory; IS curve regime transitions over decades; spaghetti at ZLB
- [[minimum-wage]] — Econ 101 wrong; Info Econ 101 simple and consistent with data; scope conditions matter
- [[nominal-shocks]] — Departure from M0-predicted NGDP path; sand pile dynamics; the empirical handle on non-ideal transfer
- [[inequality]] — Pareto reduces effective dimensionality → more variance, more coordination risk; UBI restores stability
