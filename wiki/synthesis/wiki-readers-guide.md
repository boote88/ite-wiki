## The core framework

Read these in order. Each builds on the last.

4. **[[information-transfer-model]]** — The foundation: price as a detector of information flowing from demand (source) to supply (destination). The Fielitz & Borchardt framework adapted to economics. The equation $P = (1/\kappa)(D/S)$ and what it means.

5. **[[information-equilibrium]]** — The deeper principle: maximum entropy / "ignorance equilibrium." Why assuming you know nothing about agents produces better predictions than assuming rational optimization. The gas pump analogy. Equivalence relation properties. Self-similarity between micro and macro. Markets as allocation, not information aggregation.

6. **[[supply-and-demand]]** — How S&D curves fall out of the information equilibrium condition. Scale invariance as the underlying symmetry (Noether's theorem for economics). Explicit scope conditions for when S&D applies and when it fails.

7. **[[information-transfer-index]]** — The κ parameter that governs everything: monetary policy effectiveness, regime type (QTM vs IS-LM vs liquidity trap), and the transition between them. How it changes over time and across countries. Its mathematical identity as a Lyapunov exponent.

8. **[[non-ideal-information-transfer]]** — What happens when information is lost: the inequality $I_S < I_D$. Sticky prices, recessions, market failures, and bubbles all emerge from the "orange triangle" below the ideal curves. The formal foundation for everything that goes wrong in the economy.

## The thermodynamic picture

The framework has deep parallels with physics that aren't just analogies.

9. **[[thermodynamic-analogies]]** — The price↔pressure, demand↔energy, supply↔volume mapping. From "fun to think about" (2013) to rigorous thermodynamic potentials (2015).

10. **[[entropic-forces]]** — The deepest insight. Sticky prices, wage rigidity, recessions, the hot potato effect, fiscal policy, bank runs, demand curves, the Walrasian auctioneer — all entropic forces. Macro phenomena that have no microeconomic counterpart. Causal entropic forces (Wissner-Gross & Freer) give the formal physics foundation. Fairness as entropy preference. Algorithmic randomness of complex agents.

11. **[[economic-potentials]]** — Temperature $T \sim 1/\kappa$, entropy, and thermodynamic potentials for the economy. Micro vs macro force separation. The partition function over labor markets. "Laser vs blackbody" framing of balanced growth.

12. **[[emergent-representative-agent]]** — Rational behavior emerges from high-dimensional geometry without assuming utility. Budget constraint saturation from concentration of measure. Cognitive biases as Ptolemaic epicycles. E. coli as rational utility maximizer. Two limits of human complexity (utility vs information equilibrium).

## Major applications

These pages show what the framework says about specific economic phenomena.

13. **[[information-trap]]** — When κ → 1, monetary policy dies. More general than the zero lower bound. Empirical evidence from [[japan]], [[ecb-eurozone]], Switzerland. Exit mechanisms. The provocative question: do we even want the trap to end?

14. **[[recessions]]** — Coordination failure, not demand shocks. Aperiodic avalanches, not cycles. The Great Recession complete DIEM timeline. Recovery regularity (~0.49 pp/year). COVID as twig crack on a building avalanche. Conceptions as the earliest leading indicator. Limits to wage growth hypothesis.

15. **[[inflation]]** — Two components: monetary (works only at high inflation) and demographic (labor force growth × 2.67). The DIEM shows a single 1978.7 transition (women entering workforce). The "Great Inflation" was a demographic event, not monetary.

16. **[[labor-market]]** — Two markets (wage + employment), Okun's law as mechanical identity, absolute vs relative stickiness. IE matching function replaces Mortensen-Pissarides. Racial unemployment as hysteresis. Wage growth dynamics. The labor force as the fundamental aggregate.

17. **[[interest-rates]]** — The two-rate model (long rates from M0, short from MB). Three κ regimes of interest rate dynamics. Yield curve and recession prediction.

## The money question

18. **[[money-as-bandwidth]]** — Money as information mediation via chain rule. The "money is unimportant" argument (∂R/∂M ≈ 0). **Money is the aether of macroeconomics** — the endless search for the "right" monetary aggregate mirrors the luminiferous aether.

19. **[[currency-vs-reserves]]** — M0 controls prices; MB controls rates. QE expanded the wrong aggregate. The cleanest natural experiment in macroeconomics.

20. **[[quantitative-easing]]** — Why QE doesn't cause inflation, across US, EU, and Japan.

21. **[[fiat-money-paradox]]** — Why money has value: refusing it makes YOU the sucker. Money as dissipative structure. Noether's theorem: money is the conserved quantity of scale invariance.

## The critique

22. **[[microfoundations-critique]]** — The strongest sustained argument in the wiki. Micro-details collapse to coefficients. SMD theorem as effective field theory. Four mechanisms of emergence. ABM contradiction. Dimensional reduction. Empirical evidence (prices flexible individually but sticky in aggregate). Scope conditions problem.

23. **[[expectations]]** — Why expectations are arbitrary, not central. The T0/TK thought experiment. Rational expectations fail for business cycle analysis (KL divergence proportional to nominal shocks).

24. **[[unobservables]]** — Natural rate, NAIRU, velocity, "stance of policy" — all circular constructs (phlogiston). ITM provides calculable replacements.

## The demographic thesis

25. **[[quantity-theory-of-labor]]** — NGDP in IE with civilian labor force (κ ≈ 4). Capital exponent zero. Immigration as remaining growth engine. The reductio that worked.

26. **[[phillips-curve]]** — Not a structural trade-off but mean reversion during a demographic transition. Caused by women entering the workforce. Permanently faded.

27. **[[secular-stagnation]]** — Not mysterious: larger economies are colder economies. Demographics, not policy failure.

28. **[[1990s-phase-transition]]** — The catalog of everything that changed when the demographic transition completed.

29. **[[dynamic-information-equilibrium]]** — The major empirical framework from 2017 onward. Constant log-derivative + logistic shocks. Three centuries of UK data. World population since the Neolithic. COVID-19 epidemiology. The comprehensive US history infographic.
