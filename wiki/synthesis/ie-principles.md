# Information Equilibrium as Economic Principles (2015-05-03)

[Original post](../../raw/2015/05/information-equilibrium-as-economic.md)

Smith goes through Krugman & Wells' *Macroeconomics* textbook principles and restates each in the [[information-equilibrium]] framework. The core difference: economics is solved by maximum entropy (most likely allocation), not utility optimization.

## The principles restated

| # | Krugman & Wells | Information Equilibrium |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Choices necessary because resources scarce | **Economics is the study of allocation problems** — solved by MaxEnt, not optimization |
| 2 | True cost = opportunity cost | **Unfilled states matter too** — entropy depends on what's *not* filled |
| 3 | Decisions at the margin | **P = dA/dB** — marginal thinking formally enforced by IE relationships |
| 4 | People respond to incentives | **Market forces are [[entropic-forces]]** that move people into and out of states |
| 5 | There are gains from trade | **There is an entropy term $TS$ in [[economic-potentials]]** — the whole exceeds the sum of parts |
| 6 | Markets move toward equilibrium | **Markets move toward maximum entropy** — same as thermodynamic equilibrium |
| 7 | Resources should be used efficiently | *(Kept as-is — philosophical axiom)* |
| 8 | Markets usually lead to efficiency | **$I(A) \approx I(B)$ is a good first-order approximation** — MaxEnt solutions are usually close to optima |

The "usually" in the original principles maps to [[non-ideal-information-transfer]]: sometimes $I(A) > I(B)$.

## The fundamental divide

The allocation problem can be solved by:
1. Finding the allocation that **optimizes some objective function** (utility) — the mainstream approach
2. Taking the **most likely allocation** (maximum entropy) — the information equilibrium approach

The ITM takes path 2. This is why economists haven't discovered this approach — information equilibrium abandons the human decision element and says allocation is a MaxEnt solution.
