# Exchange Rates in the ITM

The [[information-transfer-model]] derives exchange rates from relative price levels across countries.

## Derivation

Exchange rates reflect the ratio of price level functions between two economies:

$$e_{A/B} \sim \frac{P_A(M_{0,A}, \text{NGDP}_A)}{P_B(M_{0,B}, \text{NGDP}_B)}$$

A falling currency signals a relative *demand increase* (for which that currency is the information medium), not necessarily an inflation signal.

## Key insight: decoupling in the information trap

In normal regimes, exchange rate movements correlate with inflation differentials. But in the [[information-trap]], exchange rates **decouple from inflation** — a country can have a falling currency without rising inflation (or vice versa), because the price level is insensitive to monetary changes when κ → 1.
