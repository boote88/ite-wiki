# Zero Lower Bound

In the ITM's treatment of the **IS-LM model**, the IS curve (demand) transfers information to the LM curve (supply) via the interest rate. The **zero lower bound** (ZLB) represents a fundamental breakdown of this information channel.

## ITM interpretation

In the [[thermodynamic-analogies]], the ZLB is analogous to a system with **zero pressure** — it contradicts the model's assumptions (non-zero price/detector). When the interest rate hits zero, the information transfer mechanism fails: demand information can no longer reach supply.

## Policy responses (framed in ITM terms)

Smith categorizes three approaches:

1. **Keynesian**: government directly substitutes for aggregate demand (fiscal spending), bypassing the broken market mechanism until interest rates rise
2. **Monetarist**: create inflation (via expectations or QE) so the *real* interest rate goes below zero, restoring IS-LM functioning
3. **Market design**: create a new price in a new market (e.g., [[ngdp-targeting]] with futures) to replace the failed interest rate channel

Each has tradeoffs in the ITM framework. New channels start with poor information transfer capacity (see [[money-as-bandwidth]]).
