Borrowing a theme from my other blog [Spittle-Flecked Ire](http://spittlefleckedire.blogspot.com/) (which I've been ignoring), I'd like to say these statements:

> 1.  David Beckworth looks \[at\] **_a bunch_** of natural experiments on the efficacy of monetary policy at the zero bound ... [\[1\]](http://www.themoneyillusion.com/?p=23802) 
> 2.  These three quasi-natural experiments **_indicate_** that there is much monetary policy can do at the ZLB. [\[2\]](http://macromarketmusings.blogspot.com/2013/09/monetary-policy-at-zlb-three-quasi_25.html)

are based on exactly six data points across three countries. That is neither "a bunch" nor do they "indicate" anything. Here are the two data points for Japan:




![](<media/fredgraph -1-.png>)

It seems like [confirmation bias](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Confirmation_bias). I still say RGDP growth will continue its [post-2000 average for Japan](http://informationtransfereconomics.blogspot.com/2013/09/japan-interest-rates-and-liquidity-trap.html),  its [post-2009 average for the US](http://informationtransfereconomics.blogspot.com/2013/09/the-liquidity-trap-and-information-trap.html), and its [post-2009 average for the EU](http://informationtransfereconomics.blogspot.com/2013/09/analyzing-eu-with-information-transfer.html). These predictions are based on hundreds of data points (in fact, all available data).
