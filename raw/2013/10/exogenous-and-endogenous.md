In some [previous](http://informationtransfereconomics.blogspot.com/2013/09/market-monetarism-falls-in-liquidity.html) [posts](http://informationtransfereconomics.blogspot.com/2013/10/floating-information-sources-physical.html) I explained there were different models based on whether information sources and destinations were "floating" or "constant". I took these terms from the [original paper](http://arxiv.org/abs/0905.0610) which in an update (v3) has changed the language to "constant restriction" and "floating restriction". In either case, mathematically the come down to whether an integral is:





The question is whether $y$ is set inside (floating, or the second integral) or outside (constant, or the first integral) the market.



While not a precise translation from information theory to mathematics to economics, these terms are pretty close to the terms **[endogenous](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Endogeneity_\(economics\))** and [**exogenous**](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Exogeny). Therefore, the [IS-LM model](http://informationtransfereconomics.blogspot.com/2013/08/deriving-is-lm-model-from-information.html) has an exogenous aggregate demand, an endogenous aggregate supply and an endogenous money supply. Scott Sumner's\*\* model ([LS-MS](http://informationtransfereconomics.blogspot.com/2013/08/scott-sumners-model-part-2_30.html)) treats AD as endogenous in the money market but exogenous in the labor market. In the case of [accelerating inflation](http://informationtransfereconomics.blogspot.com/2013/09/hyperinflation.html), the model has an **_exogenous_** money supply and endogenous aggregate demand. In [my model](http://informationtransfereconomics.blogspot.com/2013/09/top-ten-successes-of-information.html), everything is endogenous.



Again, this is not a precise translation (see [endogeneity](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Endogeneity_\(economics\))), but in the loose sense of exogenous meaning outside the model and endogenous meaning inside the model we can switch back and forth between the two languages.



\*\* The model is not really his in the sense that he came up with it. I built it in the information transfer framework based on what he's said in his blog.
