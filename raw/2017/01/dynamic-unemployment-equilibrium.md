I thought I'd redo my [old version](http://informationtransfereconomics.blogspot.com/2016/09/eurozone-unemployment-equilibrium.html) of the EU unemployment rate using the updated [logarithmic version of the naive dynamic equilibrium](http://informationtransfereconomics.blogspot.com/2017/01/dynamic-equilibrium-unemployment-rate.html) model. However, one of the funny things that happened was that I didn't realize right off the bat that the [data at FRED](https://fred.stlouisfed.org/series/LRHUTTTTEZM156S) only goes until the end of 2013. I ended up fitting the data using just that data, so when I went to retrieve the latest data [from the ECB](http://sdw.ecb.europa.eu/home.do?chart=t1.6) (shown in black on the next graph) it ended up becoming a prediction of sorts.
