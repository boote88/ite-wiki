I realized I hadn't updated the information equilibrium (IE) model comparison with the NY Fed DSGE model and the FOMC forecast [since last August](http://informationtransfereconomics.blogspot.com/2016/08/ie-vs-ny-fed-dsge-model-update.html). Here is the most recent data (as of January 27th and 30th):



![](<media/update january 2017.png>)![](<media/update january 2017 2.png>)

The IE model is still biased low \[1\], and the NY Fed DSGE model is biased high but we still can't really reject either model.



The FOMC was still very wrong back in March of 2014.





**Footnotes:**


\[1\] One thing I noticed is that since the IE model actually starts at the beginning of 2014 it did not see the boost in employment (and therefore NGDP) [that came in mid-2014](http://informationtransfereconomics.blogspot.com/2017/01/dynamic-equilibrium-unemployment-rate.html). That might explain the biased low inflation (as NGDP is a model input).
