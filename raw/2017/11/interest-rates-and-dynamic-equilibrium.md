What if we combine an information equilibrium relationship _A ⇄ B_ with a dynamic information equilibrium description of the inputs _A_ and _B_? Say, the interest rate model ([described here](https://informationtransfereconomics.blogspot.com/2017/01/the-islm-model-reference-post.html)) with dynamic equilibrium for [investment](https://informationtransfereconomics.blogspot.com/2017/10/investment.html) and [the monetary base](https://informationtransfereconomics.blogspot.com/2017/07/causality-in-money-and-inflation-plus.html)? Turns out that it's interesting:



![](<media/dynamic equilibrium -GPDI and interest rates- 2.png>)
![](<media/dynamic equilibrium -GPDI and interest rates-.png>)

The first graph is the long term (10-year) rate and the second is the short term (3 month secondary market) rate. Green is the information equilibrium model alone (i.e. the data as input), while the gray curves show the result if we use the dynamic equilibria for [GPDI](https://fred.stlouisfed.org/series/GPDI) and [AMBSL](https://fred.stlouisfed.org/series/AMBSL) (or [CURRSL](https://fred.stlouisfed.org/series/CURRSL)) as input.
