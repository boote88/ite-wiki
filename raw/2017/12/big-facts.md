Chris Dillow has [a great post about "big facts" in economics](http://stumblingandmumbling.typepad.com/stumbling_and_mumbling/2017/12/big-facts-in-economics.html) which he illustrates with three examples. The first is the Efficient Markets Hypothesis:

> _Take, for example, the efficient markets hypothesis. Researchers have found countless small facts that seem to refute this – well over 100 anomalies at the last count. All these, however, run into the Big Fact – that fund [managers](https://www.fca.org.uk/publications/market-studies/asset-management-market-study) do [not](https://www.ft.com/content/e139d940-977d-11e6-a1dc-bdf38d484582) beat the market._

The second is involuntary unemployment:

> _Here’s a second example. A Big Fact is that the unemployed are [significantly](https://www.ons.gov.uk/peoplepopulationandcommunity/wellbeing/datasets/personalwellbeingestimatespersonalcharacteristics) less [happy](http://legacy.iza.org/en/webcontent/publications/papers/viewAbstract?dp_id=6426) than those in work. This is inconsistent with ideas that unemployment is voluntary: people should be happy if they’re on holiday. It thus [refutes](https://mainlymacro.blogspot.co.uk/2017/10/economics-too-much-ideology-too-little.html) labour market-clearing real business cycle theories._

The third is the inability to predict recessions:

> _A third Big Fact is that mainstream economic forecasters have consistently [failed](http://www.imf.org/external/pubs/cat/longres.aspx?sk=3543) to predict [recessions](https://econpapers.repec.org/paper/zbwifwkwp/1989.htm) – something which pre-dates the 2008 crisis – and in fact are much worse recession predictors than the simple yield [curve](https://ideas.repec.org/p/fip/fedfwp/2007-16.html)._

Off the top of my head, I think I'd only add [Okun's law](http://informationtransfereconomics.blogspot.com/2017/03/the-quantity-theory-of-labor-and.html) to this list. Maybe [the disappearing Phillips curve as well](https://informationtransfereconomics.blogspot.com/2017/09/was-phillips-curve-due-to-women.html). -   Unemployment dynamics [and shocks to matching functions](https://informationtransfereconomics.blogspot.com/2017/09/search-and-matching-ii-theory.html)
