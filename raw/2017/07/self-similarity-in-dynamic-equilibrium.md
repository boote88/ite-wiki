Let me say up front I am not saying the idea that stock market price time series are self-similar is new. What's new is that a specific structure (i.e. the [dynamic equilibrium](http://informationtransfereconomics.blogspot.com/2017/01/dynamic-equilibrium-presentation.html) + shocks) appears at different scales. Here we steadily zoom in on [the S&P 500](http://informationtransfereconomics.blogspot.com/2017/01/what-about-s-500.html) from a multi-year timescale, to a few years, to on the order of a year, down to months (discovering new shocks at smaller and smaller scales):
