The IE model is biased a bit low by almost as much as the DSGE model is biased high (but the former is a far simpler model than the latter). I've been tracking this forecast [since 2014](http://informationtransfereconomics.blogspot.com/2014/09/jason-versus-new-york-fed-dsge-model.html) (over three years now). There's now only one more quarter of data left. What's probably most interesting is how I've changed in looking at this model. If I were writing it down today, I'd define the model as I do in the parenthetical: a single factor production model with "money" as the factor of production.
