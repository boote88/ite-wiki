I live in Seattle and the big thing in the national news about us is that we raised our minimum wage to $15, which just went into effect for large businesses in January of this year. According to many people who oppose the minimum wage, this should have lead to disaster. Did it have an effect? Let's try and see what [the dynamic equilibrium model says](http://informationtransfereconomics.blogspot.com/2017/01/dynamic-equilibrium-unemployment-rate.html). I added a couple of extra potential shocks after the 2009 big one:



![](<media/WA unemployment dynamic equilibrium -data updates-.png>)

People who believe the minimum wage did negatively impact could probably see the negative shock centered at 2015.8 as evidence in their favor. However, that could also be the end of whatever positive shock centered at 2013.0 (which I think was hiring associated with the [ACA/Obamacare](http://informationtransfereconomics.blogspot.com/2016/08/did-aca-decrease-unemployment.html)). I showed what the path would look like if those shocks (positive and negative) were left out using a dotted line.
