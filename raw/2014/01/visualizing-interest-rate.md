Following this post on the [information transfer plucking model for the interest rate](http://informationtransfereconomics.blogspot.com/2013/12/this-plucking-model.html), I wanted to try to show the interest rate in the context of this [information transfer plucking model](http://informationtransfereconomics.blogspot.com/2013/12/plucking-rgdp-growth.html) for RGDP and inflation. Note that the interest rate and inflation rate ["interact" through the Fisher equation](http://informationtransfereconomics.blogspot.com/2013/09/the-fisher-equation-and-information.html) (I say interact because I'm a physicist and the Fisher equation reminds me of [a minimal coupling in QED](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gauge_covariant_derivative)). Eventually, I hope to be able to describe the price level and the interest rate as a particular path defined by the nominal gross domestic product (NGDP) and the monetary base (MB) across a manifold. Here is a picture of the model interest rate (black, acting as a bound as it does [here](http://informationtransfereconomics.blogspot.com/2013/12/this-plucking-model.html)) and the empirical interest rate (blue) in (logarithmic) NGDP-MB space:


![](<media/interest rate -model ceiling-.png>)

Lines of constant interest rate (gray) are shown as well. Note this maximum path is the exact same empirical path [shown in this post](http://informationtransfereconomics.blogspot.com/2013/12/plucking-rgdp-growth.html). Note that this empirical path appears to [approximate an upper bound](http://informationtransfereconomics.blogspot.com/2013/10/the-1970s.html) for e.g. RGDP on its own. It is possible this path through NGDP-MB space represents an upper bound from which RGDP (hence the inflation rate) and interest rate are deviations coupled by the Fisher equation. If I use the blue empirical interest rate path above with the [original fit to the price level](http://informationtransfereconomics.blogspot.com/2013/09/top-ten-successes-of-information.html) you can see it doesn't deviate too strongly from the empirical price level (green):
