I came across [this](http://www.federalreserve.gov/monetarypolicy/files/fomcprojtabl20140618.pdf) \[pdf\] during the course of a Google search; it shows the predictions of the Federal Reserve Board (FRB) members and the bank presidents. Maybe the predictions are based on models, but for some reason I believe the majority are just some gut opinions from some dudebros (e.g. [this guy](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Richard_W._Fisher) or [this guy](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Charles_Plosser)). Let's see how the information transfer model stands up:


![](<media/basic us price level m0 interest rates -compare FRB projections- smoothed.png>)

This is based on [the predictions here](http://informationtransfereconomics.blogspot.com/2014/09/jason-versus-fed.html), except I used a starting date of 2013 instead of 2014. The Fed guesses predictions are given as annual averages and so are shown as a coarse-graining. Note that the gray bands (70% and 90% confidence) are primarily due to [irreducible measurement error](http://informationtransfereconomics.blogspot.com/2014/07/inflation-prediction-errors.html) in the _**monthly**_ measurements -- the error in the annual averages will be much smaller. I am at a distinct disadvantage in that unlike the bank presidents I don't have a vote on the Federal Reserve Board and can't influence monetary policy to e.g. make my prediction come true.
