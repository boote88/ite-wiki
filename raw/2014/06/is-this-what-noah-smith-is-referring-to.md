[Noah Smith](http://noahpinionblog.blogspot.com/2014/06/shinzo-abe-is-actually-pretty-awesome.html)[a piece for Bloomberg View](http://www.bloombergview.com/articles/2014-06-11/japan-s-abe-is-the-world-s-best-leader)

> _\[Shinzo Abe\] went the opposite direction of Europe, and -- unlike the U.S. -- he gave every indication that the shift toward monetarism was permanent. The result: Japan has escaped deflation._



It took me awhile to [track down](http://www.e-stat.go.jp/SG1/estat/eStatTopPortalE.do) the deflation victory point (it's not yet in the data from FRED):


![](<media/japan price level update 6-13-2014.png>)

See it? It's the one point at the end above the 1-sigma model error band from April 2014. It is actually quite remarkable (a 2-sigma jump), and for the sake of the people of Japan I hope it holds up. I mentioned [in an earlier post](http://informationtransfereconomics.blogspot.com/2014/02/monetary-abenomics-has-not-generated.html) that this rise over the past few months could be due to the impact of fiscal policy. Before someone says I scaled the axes to make the jump look small, note that the US CPI traversed the entire range in the same time period (2% inflation would take you from 0.9 in 1994 to 1.1 in 2014).



A jump in CPI due to monetary policy should affect interest rates, but nothing remarkable seems to be happening. Interest rates are low given the model result (again 1-sigma error band shown):
