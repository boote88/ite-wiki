I thought this would be an interesting way to present the model results from the [previous post](http://informationtransfereconomics.blogspot.com/2014/03/modeling-macroeconomic-fluctuations.html): if we use the [effective Fed funds rate](http://www.research.stlouisfed.org/fred2/series/FEDFUNDS) instead of the 3-month rate, what we'd get is an estimate of the effective Fed target unemployment rate. Here is the graph (actual unemployment rate in green, model result in red):



![](<media/fed unemployment target.png>)

This puts a different spin on the mid-2000s peak. The Fed appears to be unsuccessfully trying to raise the unemployment rate from this perspective.
