Brad DeLong [asks](http://equitablegrowth.org/at-what-time-scale-if-any-does-the-long-run-come/): _At what time scale, if any, does the long run come?_



Some other answers besides the 3-6 years discussed by Krugman and DeLong:



-   **It's already here** in the IT model (we're back on trend, just not the trend you were thinking of [\[1\]](http://informationtransfereconomics.blogspot.com/2015/01/ngdp-predictions-and-new-normal.html), [\[2\]](http://informationtransfereconomics.blogspot.com/2015/10/the-paradigm-dependence-of-productivity.html)). The time scale governing [nominal shocks](http://informationtransfereconomics.blogspot.com/2015/08/employment-doesnt-depend-of-inflation.html) is really short; the time scale for [unemployment is a bit longer](http://informationtransfereconomics.blogspot.com/2014/07/remarkable-recovery-regularity-and.html) (probably governed by [matching theory](http://informationtransfereconomics.blogspot.com/2014/03/information-transfer-and-cobb-douglas.html)).
-   **Never** if the current state is defined by [expectations at infinity](http://informationtransfereconomics.blogspot.com/2015/11/temporal-shapes-of-discount-factors-and.html) (unless we change them). This (oddly) encompasses the liquidity trap and neo-Fisher views.
-   **Whenever** the central bank [really wants it](http://informationtransfereconomics.blogspot.com/2015/11/does-market-monetarism-exist-in.html) to come (Green Lantern theory).
