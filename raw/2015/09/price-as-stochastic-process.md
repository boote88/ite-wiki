I mentioned the other day that I'd like to look into the Black Scholes equation in terms of information equilibrium. John Cochrane's _Asset Pricing_ (2005) derives it using the asset pricing equation and a bit of stochastic calculus. I already looked at the basic asset pricing equation [here](http://informationtransfereconomics.blogspot.com/2015/05/the-basic-asset-pricing-equation-as.html), so I'm just going to set up the price in the information transfer framework as a stochastic process.

































Which is a geometric stochastic process that would become the price $p$ of the underlying stock in the Black-Scholes option pricing model.
