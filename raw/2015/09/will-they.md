Will the Fed raise rates or keep them where they are (actually [keep a 0.25% ceiling](https://research.stlouisfed.org/fred2/series/DFEDTARU))?



Who knows? Tim Duy seems [knowledgeable](http://economistsview.typepad.com/economistsview/2015/09/fed-watch-final-thoughts-on-september.html). But in our universe we put these things to a vote by a couple dozen people so the result should essentially be dominated by noise ... because nobody knows what they are doing. It's all randomness.



[All I can tell you](http://informationtransfereconomics.blogspot.com/2014/11/quantitative-easing-cleanest-experiment.html) is that if rates rise, the monetary base will fall. And pretty much nothing else. The stock market might fall. But then the market might take a rate rise as confidence in the future and go up. Who knows? The markets have no idea what they are doing either.



It's all [dither](http://informationtransfereconomics.blogspot.com/2015/02/jaynes-on-entropy-in-economics.html). We're grappling around an abstract space following entropy gradients. Excuse me, I should say it's all tâtonnement. It's a serious thing and we should use serious technical jargon.
