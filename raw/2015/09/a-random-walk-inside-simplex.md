I thought I'd do something similar to [this post](http://informationtransfereconomics.blogspot.com/2015/09/price-movements.html) for the idea behind these two posts ([\[1\]](http://informationtransfereconomics.blogspot.com/2015/09/the-emergent-representative-agent-1.html), [\[2\]](http://informationtransfereconomics.blogspot.com/2015/06/34-of-knife-34-of-fork-and-34-of-spoon.html)): that most of the points in a high dimensional space are near the boundary. Here is an example random walk over _d_ \= 3 goods (3 dimensions) restricted to the simplex bounded by the budget constraint _Σ ci_ ≤ 1:



![](<media/random walk in simplex.png>)

And here is the resulting "total consumption", i.e. the sum of the consumption of each good (1 means the ensemble of agents spends all of their money in that time period):



![](<media/random walk in simplex 2.png>)

If you increase the number of goods to _d_ = 20, you get a similar result, but closer to the boundary:



![](<media/random walk in simplex 3.png>)

The deviations from _Σ ci_ = 1 represent "recessions" where the ensemble of agents saved (reduced consumption) more than usual.
