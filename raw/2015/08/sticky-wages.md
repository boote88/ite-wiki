I'm not sure I understand how [economists](http://macroblog.typepad.com/macroblog/2015/08/no-wage-change.html) (including [Mark Thoma](http://economistsview.typepad.com/economistsview/2015/08/evidence-for-sticky-wages.html)) can look at this data:



[![](media/6a00d8341c834f53ef01b7c7c27a51970b-500wi.jpg)](http://macroblog.typepad.com/.a/6a00d8341c834f53ef01b7c7c27a51970b-500wi)

... and say:

> _Taken at face value, this analysis suggests the presence of some amount of wage rigidity._

24% of people are reporting nominal hourly wage declines. Only 20% are reporting the same wage. **That means something like 80% of people are reporting wage changes.** They should check out the section of [my paper](http://informationtransfereconomics.blogspot.com/2015/08/information-equilibrium-as-economic.html) on entropic forces and nominal rigidity. Or [this post](http://informationtransfereconomics.blogspot.com/2015/04/micro-stickiness-versus-macro-stickiness.html) on macro rigidity and micro flexibility.
