So it was desk rejection from the [Economics e-journal](http://www.economics-ejournal.org/) for [the preprint](http://informationtransfereconomics.blogspot.com/2015/08/information-equilibrium-as-economic.html). Probably more of those to be expected given I'm not officially an economist by degree ... but I am a member of the AEA -- doesn't that count? It probably would have gone to my head (in a bad way) had it made it through peer review without a hitch.



It's on the arXiv and also apparently at EconPapers for those that are interested (and/or want to spread it around ... ):

I am somewhat partial to Igor Carron's [idea of a public peer review process](http://nuit-blanche.blogspot.com/2011/11/to-serve-science.html) ... as I mentioned in my [first blog post](http://informationtransfereconomics.blogspot.com/2013/04/an-informal-abstract.html):

> _Instead of trying (and probably failing) to publish_ \[information transfer model of supply and demand\] _as a paper, I was inspired by [Igor Carron](http://nuit-blanche.blogspot.com/) to just think out loud with a blog. This blog will be focused on determining if the framework established here is good for anything or just an interesting toy model. Or if it is completely wrong!_
