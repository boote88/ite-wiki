I'd like to re-iterate a comment I made in [the last post](http://informationtransfereconomics.blogspot.com/2015/03/the-long-run-in-uk-redux.html): the information transfer model doesn't explain everything on its own, There are some obvious and genuine effects of the oil price spikes in [1973](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/1973_oil_crisis) and [1979](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/1979_energy_crisis) that the model doesn't capture. That is what happens when a particular market (in this case, oil) becomes important and you can't just assume [thousands of random markets](http://informationtransfereconomics.blogspot.com/2014/06/the-macroeconomic-partition-function.html).
