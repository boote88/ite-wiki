In [this post](http://informationtransfereconomics.blogspot.com/2015/03/the-long-run-in-uk-redux.html) I just did a bit of educated guessing about where the monetary regime breaks should go over the past 300+ years in the UK (time series from [here](http://www.bankofengland.co.uk/research/Pages/onebank/datasets.aspx)). Turns out all but one (in 1790) roughly correspond to points where interest rates changed from being/not being "pegged" (see [here](http://informationtransfereconomics.blogspot.com/2015/04/will-uk-be-first-to-exit-great-recession.html), [here](http://informationtransfereconomics.blogspot.com/2015/10/pegged-interest-rates-hyperinflation.html), and [here](http://informationtransfereconomics.blogspot.com/2015/10/interest-rate-dynamics.html)):
