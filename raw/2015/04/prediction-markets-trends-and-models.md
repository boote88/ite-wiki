New NGDP numbers are going [to come out soon](http://www.bea.gov/newsreleases/news_release_sort_national.htm) (at the end of April) and so I've updated [this prediction](http://informationtransfereconomics.blogspot.com/2015/01/ngdp-predictions-and-new-normal.html) and put the Hypermind predictions ([linked at Scott Sumner's blog](http://www.themoneyillusion.com/)) on my prediction. Lo and behold there really will be no way to distinguish among the prediction market (black line segments), the post-recession trend (solid red) the long term trend (dashed red) and the information equilibrium model (gray):



![](<media/NGDP path.png>)

My view of prediction markets is [here](http://informationtransfereconomics.blogspot.com/2015/01/is-market-intelligent.html) (they seem to only be asynchronous polls).



PS [Scott Sumner says](http://www.themoneyillusion.com/?p=29133) that the prediction market believes [the Great Stagnation](http://informationtransfereconomics.blogspot.com/2014/09/the-great-stagnation-information.html). Couldn't we say the market believes the information equilibrium model?
