And the [new GDP data](http://www.bea.gov/newsreleases/news_release_sort_national.htm) is out! A pretty dismal performance ... but then the Atlanta Fed was saying Q1 seems to [come in low these days](http://www.pragcap.com/three-things-i-think-i-think-36) (H/T to Cullen Roche who also had a good prediction), and in fact pretty much nailed the number with their [GDPNow forecast](https://www.frbatlanta.org/cqer/researchcq/gdpnow.cfm).



![](<media/NGDP path -new data-.png>)

I marked the Q1 numbers with blue dots on [the prediction graph](http://informationtransfereconomics.blogspot.com/2015/04/ngdp-prediction-updates.html) -- they do seem to have been systematically low since the financial crisis.



Overall: a continuing a trend of lukewarm economic performance, largely still in line with just about any model of the economy.
