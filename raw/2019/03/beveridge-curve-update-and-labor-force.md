I don't think I've updated the (traditional) Beveridge curve [for almost a year](https://informationtransfereconomics.blogspot.com/2018/08/jolts-data-and-counterfactual-2019.html), so here you go:



![](<media/dynamic equilibrium -beveridge unrate openings- data updates.png>)

The details behind this plot (in terms of the dynamic information equilibrium model, DIEM) are actually [in my paper](https://papers.ssrn.com/sol3/papers.cfm?abstract_id=3094757) (it's Figure 3.6). It's first appearance on this blog [was in October of 2017](https://informationtransfereconomics.blogspot.com/2017/10/the-beveridge-curve.html). [A couple of weeks later](https://informationtransfereconomics.blogspot.com/2017/11/a-new-beveridge-curve-or-science-is.html) I speculated about the existence of another Beveridge-like curve relating labor force participation (ages 25-54) and unemployment which also seems to be working out well:



![](<media/dynamic equilibrium -clf part and unrate- data updates.png>)
