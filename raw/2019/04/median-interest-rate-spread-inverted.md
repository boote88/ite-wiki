We're at edge of the two sigma band where a recession occurs after the monthly median interest rate data manifests a yield curve inversion. The median _daily_ interest rate data showed a brief inversion at the end of March — about two weeks ago:



![](<media/interest rate spreads.png>)

The gray band is where the [interest rate spread indicator](https://informationtransfereconomics.blogspot.com/2018/06/yield-curve-inversion-and-future.html) points to a recession based on a simple linear extrapolation (blue)/AR process (red) based on median (which in this case [is basically equal to the principal component](https://informationtransfereconomics.blogspot.com/2018/06/yield-curve-inversion-and-future.html)) of [multiple spreads](https://fred.stlouisfed.org/graph/?graph_id=482141&rn=775).



Note that except for the 2001 recession, these median rate spreads started to head back up by this point over the past few recessions — reminding us that we will probably see the Fed lower rates (increasing the short-long rate spread) before a recession starts. Or maybe the yield curve will prove to be a false alarm — to be placed on the scrapheap of indicators that lose their predictive ability as soon as they're widely recognized.
