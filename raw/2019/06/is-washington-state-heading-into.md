Back in 2017, [I looked at Washington State's unemployment rate](https://informationtransfereconomics.blogspot.com/2017/03/washingtons-unemployment-rate-seattles.html) to see if Seattle's minimum wage laws had impacted employment. However, I haven't checked back in with that forecast since that time and when I did, there appears to be a spike in unemployment. Is a recession brewing?



![](<media/WA unemployment dynamic equilibrium -data updates-.png>)

Click to enlarge. It doesn't quite reach the [threshold for a recession](https://informationtransfereconomics.blogspot.com/2019/06/market-updates-fairs-model-and-sahms.html). We have δlog _u_ ~ 0.16, not yet 0.17 — which in any case is the threshold for a national recession based on national data, not state data which fluctuates more. However, [other data suggests](https://informationtransfereconomics.blogspot.com/2019/05/economic-weakness-in-west.html) there might be economic weakness in the West census region[. Therefore, this seems like a good time series to monitor. (As a side note,](https://fred.stlouisfed.org/series/SEXRSA) Seattle's housing prices seem to have [hit a hiccup](https://informationtransfereconomics.blogspot.com/2018/12/imagine-theres-no-bubble.html).)
