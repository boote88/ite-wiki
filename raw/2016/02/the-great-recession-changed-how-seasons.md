There was an uptick in inflation with the January 2016 numbers that some have considered to be noteworthy (e.g. [here](https://twitter.com/dandolfa/status/703302043071418368) -- although that is a year-over-year measure, that John Handley takes on [here](http://ramblingsofanamateureconomist.blogspot.com/2016/02/dont-be-fooled-by-annual-inflation.html) -- that it's most likely due to the very low Jan 2015 number).



I wanted to see if it had something to do with seasonal adjustment (since this was floated as an explanation of why the Q1 GDP has been so low -- q.v. [here](http://bruegel.org/2015/05/the-residual-seasonality-puzzle/)). CPI inflation shows the same jump (see my table I've been comparing to the IT model [here](http://informationtransfereconomics.blogspot.com/2015/11/cpi-inflation-predictions-and.html)) and unlike PCE has both seasonally adjusted and unadjusted data on FRED. ![](<media/seasonal adjustment weirdness 3.png>)

There is no particular reason that this affects January more than other months -- actually January is one of the few months that wasn't strongly affected by the Great Recession. Maybe the January CPI number isn't big -- it's just that the other monthly numbers got small.
