Continuing this series ([\[1\]](http://informationtransfereconomics.blogspot.com/2016/10/parsing-macrohistory-database-interest.html), [\[2\]](http://informationtransfereconomics.blogspot.com/2016/10/parsing-macrohistory-database-principal.html)), I did a principal component analysis of long term interest rates (in \[2\], it was short term interest rates) in the [macrohistory database](http://www.macrohistory.net/data/#DownloadData) \[0\] and came to roughly the same conclusion: the US is the dominant component vector. Interestingly, the distant runner up is actually Finland. It seems Finland's interest rate history is unique enough to warrant its own component.
