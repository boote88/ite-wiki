When I issued [some predictions two years ago](http://informationtransfereconomics.blogspot.com/2014/03/macroeconomic-predictions-for-2016.html) (that turned out [well](http://informationtransfereconomics.blogspot.com/2016/04/celebrate-this-blogs-birthday-with.html)), I put up a speculative piece about what I called "metastability" in the unemployment rate. It was pure speculation that just came from looking at the unemployment rate graph:



![](<media/unrate 1 -predictions-.png>)

Well, it turns out it is wrong. In the original data, there was (almost) never a case where unemployment went through the red or blue metastable level without pausing at it (it did fall through the blue level in the 1950s without pausing).
