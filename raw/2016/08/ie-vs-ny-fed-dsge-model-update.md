I haven't updated the head-to-head with the NY Fed DSGE model -- by that I mean the 2014 vintage of that model. The model and the forecast has been [changed several times](http://informationtransfereconomics.blogspot.com/2015/12/ny-fed-dsge-model-update.html) since 2014, including [in May of this year](http://libertystreeteconomics.newyorkfed.org/2016/05/the-frbny-dsge-model-forecastmay-2016.html#.V7tyMfkrKih) a month after [the last update from me](http://informationtransfereconomics.blogspot.com/2016/04/update-to-2014-it-model-inflation.html). The model now only projects a year ahead (as opposed to the nearly 4 years of the original vintage 2014 model).



![](<media/update 3.jpg>)

And the saddest part? The original 2014 vintage of the model is doing an amazing job! The core PCE inflation data has been heavily revised \[1\] and what previously looked like a blow out for the IE model has turned into a slight advantage for the vintage 2014 DSGE model.



![](<media/basic us price level compare FRB-US model.png>)

Still, we'll probably have to wait until the beginning of 2017 to know which model is better. This is consistent with [the expected performance](http://informationtransfereconomics.blogspot.com/2014/07/inflation-prediction-errors.html) of the IE model: observation times shorter than a few years are dominated by irreducible measurement error.
