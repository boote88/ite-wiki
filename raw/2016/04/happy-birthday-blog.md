Yesterday was this blog's 3rd birthday. The real celebration will come at the end of this week with the release of GDP data from the BEA. In the meantime, there are a couple of updates:



[For the book](http://informationtransfereconomics.blogspot.com/2016/04/a-random-physicist-takes-on-economics.html), I've reached 10,000 words -- a third of my 30,000 word goal. It's turning into a pretty strong indictment of economics in general.



On the blog, I updated [the comparative advantage post](http://informationtransfereconomics.blogspot.com/2016/04/comparative-advantage-from-maximum.html) which has gotten a bit more interesting.

Here's a list of the top posts (by pageviews) of all time:



**1\. [More like stock-flow inconsistent](http://informationtransfereconomics.blogspot.com/2016/03/more-like-stock-flow-in-consistent.html)**

Mar 3, 2016



Not only did my criticism of the Stock-Flow Consistent (SFC) approach top the pageviews, it topped the list of the greatest number of comments (at over 200).



**2\. [This analysis is so bad](http://informationtransfereconomics.blogspot.com/2015/06/this-analysis-is-so-bad.html)**

Jun 19, 2015



My criticism of Scott Sumner's "proof" of monetary offset and that fiscal stimulus has no impact also kicked up a hornets' nest.



**3\. [Mark Thoma, information equilibrium is the model your're looking for](http://informationtransfereconomics.blogspot.com/2015/04/mark-thoma-information-equilibrium-is.html)**

Apr 7, 2015



I tried to get a quick summary in front of Mark Thoma because he had just written an article that seemed to be a RFP for the information equilibrium model.



**4\. [The irony of Paul Romer's mathiness](http://informationtransfereconomics.blogspot.com/2015/05/the-irony-of-paul-romers-mathiness.html)**

Paul Romer makes the same mistake Robert Lucas makes, which began my slow realization that economists don't understand that [infinity is dimensionless](http://informationtransfereconomics.blogspot.com/2015/11/on-limits.html).



**5\. [It's people. The economy is made out of people.](http://informationtransfereconomics.blogspot.com/2016/01/its-people-economy-is-made-out-of-people.html)**
