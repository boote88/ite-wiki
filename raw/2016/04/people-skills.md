As Noah has shifted from criticism of economics to defense of the status quo (becoming part of it as an Assistant Professor at Stony Brook \[nice school; did a talk there on my postdoc search\]), his defenses have gotten a bit more strained (e.g. [here](http://informationtransfereconomics.blogspot.com/2016/04/economic-imperialism.html)). Take this for example:

> More tech companies are hiring economists: [https://t.co/2cTDUVeool](https://t.co/2cTDUVeool) Because there's lots of econ that actually, you know, \*works\*.
>
> — Noah Smith (@Noahpinion) [April 8, 2016](https://twitter.com/Noahpinion/status/718501274350555136)



But according to the article:

> _Much of \[economists'\] value to tech firms is in helping to connect the engineering side with the business side._ 

> _“Economists are trained in the intuition and goals of business, but they are also comfortable with data, statistics and the technical aspects of running a business,” Athey said. “A successful economist is multilingual.”_
