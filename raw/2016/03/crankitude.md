I get dismissed as either a crank or not enough of a crank, which makes me feel like I have just the right amount of crankitude (crankiness is taken, but also applies). But I'd like to put together a list of results and concepts where information equilibrium (IE) matches up with elementary economics and the writings of professional economists with real credentials.



**Marginalism**



At the heart of the IE framework is [marginal thinking](http://informationtransfereconomics.blogspot.com/2014/08/fishers-proto-information-transfer.html). The basic equation I use is a slight generalization of one that appears in Irving Fisher's thesis that comes from Jevons and Marshall. This can even be translated into [marginal utility](http://informationtransfereconomics.blogspot.com/2015/03/utility-in-information-equilibrium-model.html). If you can dismiss marginalism, then you can dismiss the IE framework.



**Supply and demand**



If you strip the IE model of its information theory interpretation, it is [formally a theory of supply and demand](http://informationtransfereconomics.blogspot.com/2015/04/information-theory-and-economics-primer.html).  If you think you can dismiss supply and demand, then you can dismiss the IE framework.



**Basic principles of economics**



The IE framework is not very different from [textbook principles of economics](http://informationtransfereconomics.blogspot.com/2015/05/information-equilibrium-as-economic.html).



**Rational utility maximizing agents (but don't have to be)**



[Agents can (collectively)](http://informationtransfereconomics.blogspot.com/2015/09/the-emergent-representative-agent-1.html) smooth consumption and have stable, transitive preferences. [Agents don't undo gains](http://informationtransfereconomics.blogspot.com/2016/03/the-entropy-term-and-unreasonable.html). All of these things can fail as well (e.g. when not in equilibrium) -- so it doesn't exclude behavioral economics. As long as you are in a macro equilibrium ("[macrofoundations](http://informationtransfereconomics.blogspot.com/2016/02/one-more-physics-analogy.html)"), rational agents can be used to explain perturbations around it.



**Euler equation**



IE can be used to arrive at [the Euler equation](http://informationtransfereconomics.blogspot.com/2015/06/the-euler-equation-as-maximum-entropy.html), assuming equilibrium ("macrofoundations").



**Asset pricing equation**



You can obtain the [asset pricing equation](http://informationtransfereconomics.blogspot.com/2015/05/the-basic-asset-pricing-equation-as.html) John Cochrane uses as the basis for his approach to economics. Again, assuming equilibrium ("macrofoundations").



**The changing slope of the Phillips curve and secular stagnation**



Essentially the IE model gives the same result ([here](http://informationtransfereconomics.blogspot.com/2016/01/the-slope-of-phillips-curve-is-roughly.html) or [here](http://informationtransfereconomics.blogspot.com/2015/09/the-phillips-curve-and-information.html)) as a paper by Olivier Blanchard, Eugenio Cerutti, and Lawrence Summers. Also, there's [secular stagnation](http://informationtransfereconomics.blogspot.com/2014/09/the-great-stagnation-information.html).



**The liquidity trap**



The IE model can recover some of the [basic properties of the liquidity trap](http://informationtransfereconomics.blogspot.com/2014/06/krugman-keynes-and-liquidity-trap.html). The underlying mechanism is different, but the outcome is the same. The [IS-LM model](http://informationtransfereconomics.blogspot.com/2016/02/the-is-lm-model-as-effective-theory-at.html) is an approximation at low inflation. Generally, [this blog agrees with Paul Krugman](http://informationtransfereconomics.blogspot.com/2014/11/the-information-transfer-model-and.html).



**The impacts of monetary policy on interest rates**



The model contains many of the [basic effects that impact interest rates](http://informationtransfereconomics.blogspot.com/2014/03/the-effects-that-move-interest-rates.html). The interest rate model is also consistent with e.g. [this analysis (by John Cochrane) of interest rates and inflation](http://informationtransfereconomics.blogspot.com/2015/11/principal-component-information.html).
