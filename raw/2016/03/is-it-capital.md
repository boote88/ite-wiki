[the model here](http://informationtransfereconomics.blogspot.com/2016/03/a-quantity-theory-of-labor-and-capital.html)[actual deflator](https://research.stlouisfed.org/fred2/series/PLKCPPUSA670NRUG)[real capital time series](https://research.stlouisfed.org/fred2/series/RKNANPUSA666NRUG)


However, ever since I put that model together I asked myself: does it have to be capital? What else could Z be in the model:



_CPI : NGDP ⇄ L_
   _X : NGDP ⇄ Z_


Here's the graph of nominal capital alongside nominal wages (times a normalization factor):



![](<media/nom cap and wages.png>)

They have about the same shape -- maybe that would work just as well for _Z_? Or maybe some measure of money? It turns out those choices don't work. The problem is that they have too much of the business cycle in them making nominal growth fluctuate too much and inflation jump around.



It also looks like NGDP, but _Z_ \= NGDP is problematic for obvious reasons. It's also not smooth enough.



Note that it's not just the annual data smoothing out the results -- annual data doesn't smooth out wages or money enough to work. Nominal capital is smoother than wages or money.



So what else could it be? [Here's the FRED series](https://research.stlouisfed.org/fred2/graph/?g=3Pcu) (real capital multiplied by price level of capital). Is there anything that is comparable?
